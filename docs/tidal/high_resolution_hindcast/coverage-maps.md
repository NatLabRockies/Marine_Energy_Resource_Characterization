# Coverage Maps

Spatial footprint of each tidal hindcast location. Each map shows the extent of the FVCOM unstructured triangular grid used for that location's simulation.

## Aleutian Islands, Alaska

- **Grid Points**: 797,978
- **Hindcast Period**: 2010-06-03 to 2011-06-02
- **Sampling**: Hourly

The Aleutian Islands domain covers the passes and channels between the islands where strong tidal currents are driven by large tidal height differences between the Pacific Ocean and the Bering Sea.

## Cook Inlet, Alaska

- **Grid Points**: 392,002
- **Hindcast Period**: 2005-01-01 to 2005-12-31
- **Sampling**: Hourly

The Cook Inlet domain covers the upper and lower inlet, including areas with some of the strongest tidal currents in the United States (tidal ranges exceeding 9 meters in some areas).

## Piscataqua River, New Hampshire

- **Grid Points**: 292,927
- **Hindcast Period**: 2007-01-01 to 2007-12-31
- **Sampling**: Half-hourly

The Piscataqua River domain covers the tidal river and its approaches, including the constriction between Portsmouth, NH and Kittery, ME.

## Puget Sound, Washington

- **Grid Points**: 1,734,765
- **Hindcast Period**: 2015-01-01 to 2015-12-30
- **Sampling**: Half-hourly

The Puget Sound domain is the largest in the dataset, covering the complex inland waterway system including Admiralty Inlet, the Tacoma Narrows, and the San Juan Islands passages.

## Western Passage, Maine

- **Grid Points**: 231,208
- **Hindcast Period**: 2017-01-01 to 2017-12-31
- **Sampling**: Half-hourly

The Western Passage domain covers the passage between Eastport, ME and Deer Island, NB (Canada), one of the strongest tidal current locations on the U.S. East Coast.
